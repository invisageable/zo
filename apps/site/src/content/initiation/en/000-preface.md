# preface

[tsh-tsh]

Where do I even start? I'm out of words... this whole topic just drives me up the wall. I don't know about you, but this digital sham is getting more broken by the day. Take the tools we use every day: compilers, test runners, code editors — most of them are dead by the opening credits, even if their creators try to sell you "Don't worry, it's fast!" — and then you actually run the thing and realize the dream was just lulling you to sleep.

Now, on top of that, they serve us user interfaces that look like a pack of bashi-bazouks. Reminder — these are the tools they force on us every single day. You wake up in the morning and BAM you're stuck using a back-office that hasn't been maintained in years. No building manager, no super, the facade plastered in pigeon shit.

Every interface looks the same, as if the creatives had been tossed out of every company like Jazzy Jeff. This uniformization of design, of innovation, is brutal to live with. Back in '76, in the nineteen-hundreds, the two Steves (Jobs and Wozniak) understood that creatives and technicians had to work hand in glove for the sake of craft — to ship things ahead of their time.

Times change, so it's better to keep moving forward. That's why I decided to follow in the footsteps of my peers. T/F Jonathan Blow, Graydon Hoare, Mike Acton, Bret Victor and my compilords. That's why I built zo — like them, I'm tired and sick of putting up with it.

This initiation will let you see there's another realm of possibilities waiting for us, and we don't have to let the mainstream walk all over us. It's first and foremost a question of independence.
